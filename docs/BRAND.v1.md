# BRAND.md — Estate One Group brand voice & art direction

## The brand in one sentence

> Estate One Group is a discreet, founder-led luxury real estate firm in South Florida that treats the home as the most personal investment of all.

## Brand pillars

1. **Discretion.** We don't shout. We don't list every property publicly. Off-market access is a feature, not a side note.
2. **Architectural literacy.** We understand buildings. Year built, architect, structural integrity, materials — these matter to us and to our clients.
3. **Founder-led.** Adiel Figueroa and his co-founder are the brand. Not a faceless agency.
4. **Bilingual fluency.** Miami is bilingual. So are we. The site speaks both EN and ES with equal polish — never machine-translated.
5. **Long-term.** We are not chasing transactions. We are building a family-office-grade relationship with each client.

## What we are NOT

- We are not a portal. (We do not compete with Zillow, Realtor.com, or Redfin.)
- We are not a high-volume firm. (We close 12–24 transactions a year, not 200.)
- We are not lifestyle marketers. (We do not sell "Miami glamour" — we sell properties that hold value across decades.)
- We are not budget. (Our floor is roughly $1M; most listings are $2M+.)

## Voice & tone

| Attribute | We are | We are NOT |
|---|---|---|
| Pace | Slow, deliberate | Urgent, FOMO-driven |
| Vocabulary | Precise, architectural | Generic real-estate buzzwords |
| Sentences | Short. Confident. Editorial. | Long, hedged, marketing-fluffy |
| Pronouns | "Our clients", "the residence" | "You'll love this!", "Don't miss out!" |
| CTAs | "Schedule a private tour" | "Get instant access!" |
| Formality (EN) | Refined American | Stuffy British |
| Formality (ES) | Usted, neutro internacional | Tú casual, regionalismos |

### Words we use

- *Residence* (instead of "house" or "property")
- *The collection* (instead of "our listings")
- *Curated* (used sparingly)
- *Architecturally distinguished*
- *Off-market*
- *Stewardship*
- *Discretion*
- *Provenance* (for older homes)
- *Legacy*

### Words we never use

- *Luxury* in headlines (overused — show, don't tell). It's fine in metadata.
- *Stunning*, *gorgeous*, *amazing*, *incredible* — adjective inflation
- *Dream home*
- *Hot listing*
- *Exclusive opportunity* (every listing is implicitly exclusive)
- *Don't miss*, *limited time*, *act now*
- *Properties* in the H1 of a listing page (use *residence*)
- Emoji of any kind

## Copy formulas

### Property descriptions (4-paragraph editorial structure)

1. **Aspirational opening (2–3 sentences).** What does it feel like to live there? Engage senses, not specs.
2. **Architectural and material specifics (2–3 sentences).** What is special about the building? Year, architect, materials, distinctive features.
3. **Lifestyle and location (2–3 sentences).** What does this neighborhood offer? What kind of life is possible here?
4. **Value & legacy close (1–2 sentences).** Why does this hold value? What does ownership mean here?

**Example (EN):**
> Sunlight enters Villa Aurora through floor-to-ceiling glass that disappears into the walls — a deliberate choice by architect Chad Oppenheim, who designed the residence to dissolve the boundary between interior and the Atlantic.
>
> Built in 2021 with a poured-concrete shell and Italian travertine throughout, the home spans 5,800 square feet across two levels, including a 60-foot infinity edge pool that reads as continuous with the ocean horizon.
>
> Sunny Isles Beach has become a quiet alternative to South Beach for owners who value privacy. Bal Harbour Shops, the K-12 RASG Hebrew Academy, and a private marina are within four minutes.
>
> Properties of this provenance trade rarely. Villa Aurora is offered for the first time since its completion.

### Hero headlines (rule of three)

A great hero headline does three things:
1. Names a feeling, not a feature
2. Uses 4–7 words
3. Reads well in italic Playfair

Approved:
- *Where luxury becomes home.*
- *The home is the most personal investment of all.*
- *South Florida, considered.*
- *Provenance you can come home to.*

Rejected:
- *Find your dream home today!* (cliché, urgent)
- *Premium luxury real estate in Miami* (SEO-stuffed)
- *We sell exceptional homes* (we-centric, not aspirational)

### Eyebrows (the small label above the headline)

10px, uppercase, tracking 0.25em, gold-deep on cream / gold on dark.

Approved patterns:
- `MIAMI · SOUTH FLORIDA`
- `THE COLLECTION`
- `OFF-MARKET ACCESS`
- `SELECTED RESIDENCES`

## Bilingual policy

### Translation philosophy

We do not translate word-for-word. We **transcreate** — every string in ES is independently crafted to feel native to a Spanish-speaking Miami resident, not translated from English.

The Spanish version uses **usted** (formal), neutral international Spanish (avoid Mexican, Argentine, or Cuban regionalisms). The exception is real estate-specific vocabulary, which uses the standard Miami market usage (e.g. *casa* not *vivienda*, *condominio* not *piso*).

### Tagline pairs

| EN | ES |
|---|---|
| Where luxury becomes home | Donde el lujo se convierte en hogar |
| Schedule a private tour | Agende una visita privada |
| Explore the Collection | Explore la colección |
| Off-market access | Acceso fuera de mercado |
| Selected residences | Residencias seleccionadas |
| Schedule consultation | Agende una asesoría |

### Fallback rule

If a piece of content has no ES equivalent yet, the site shows the EN version with a small `[EN]` indicator. **Never machine-translate.**

## Visual art direction

### What our photography looks like

- **Architecture-first:** The building is the protagonist. People are absent or implied.
- **Time of day:** Golden hour or blue hour. No harsh midday sun.
- **Composition:** Off-center, asymmetric, with negative space.
- **Color treatment:** Warm tones favored. Slight crush in shadows. Never oversaturated.

### What our photography is NOT

- Smiling agents in suits
- Realtor signs in front of houses
- Drone shots that look like Google Earth
- HDR-blended interior shots that look fake
- Couples touring with realtors
- Stock photography of "luxury"

### Until we have real photography

Use:
- Solid gradient placeholders in brand colors (charcoal → gold-deep) — see PropertyCard placeholder spec in DESIGN.md
- Architectural line drawings as fillers
- The Estate One triangle isotype as a watermark on placeholder cards

Do NOT use Unsplash for production. Mark all Unsplash usage with `// TODO: replace with commissioned photography`.

## When the brand contradicts a stakeholder request

If someone (the founder, a marketer, an investor) asks for something that contradicts this brand document — **flag it, don't just comply**. Examples:

- Request: "Add a popup with a discount code"
  - Response: "That conflicts with our discretion principle. Could we instead [alternative]?"
- Request: "Use Unsplash photos for the launch"
  - Response: "Acceptable as a temporary placeholder, marked in code as TODO. Not for the public launch."
- Request: "Add testimonials from old clients (paraphrased)"
  - Response: "Testimonials must be real and attributed. If we can't get permission, we don't show fabricated ones."

The brand is the long-term asset. Short-term requests should bend to it, not the other way.
