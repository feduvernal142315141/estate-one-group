# BRAND.md — Estate One Group brand voice & art direction

> Source of truth for all copy and voice decisions. If a component or piece of copy disagrees with this file, the copy is wrong.

---

## The brand in one sentence

> Estate One Group is a founder-led, modern real estate firm in South Florida with a direct, considered approach to premium homes.

---

## Brand pillars

1. **Direct.** We give clear information. We do not hide behind euphemisms or aspirational fog.
2. **Architectural literacy.** We understand buildings. Year built, architect, structural integrity, materials — these matter to us and to our clients.
3. **Founder-led.** Adiel Figueroa is the brand. Not a faceless agency.
4. **Bilingual fluency.** Miami is bilingual. So are we. The site speaks both EN and ES with equal polish — never machine-translated.
5. **Long-term.** We are not chasing transactions. We build lasting relationships with buyers and sellers who value precision over volume.

---

## What we are NOT

- We are not a portal. (We do not compete with Zillow, Realtor.com, or Redfin.)
- We are not a high-volume firm. (We close 12–24 transactions a year, not 200.)
- We are not lifestyle marketers. (We do not sell "Miami glamour" — we sell properties that hold value across decades.)
- We are not budget. (Our floor is roughly $1M; most listings are $2M+.)

---

## Voice & tone

| Attribute | We are | We are NOT |
|---|---|---|
| Pace | Steady, direct | Urgent, FOMO-driven |
| Vocabulary | Precise, architectural | Generic real-estate buzzwords or heritage-catalog language |
| Sentences | Short. Confident. Clear. | Long, hedged, marketing-fluffy |
| Pronouns | "Our clients", "the home" | "You'll love this!", "Don't miss out!" |
| CTAs | "Schedule a tour" | "Schedule a Private Showing!" |
| Formality (EN) | Modern American, informal-professional | Stuffy British or overly formal |
| Formality (ES) | Usted, neutro internacional | Tú casual, regionalismos |

The voice is closer to **Linear's product writing** than to a Sotheby's catalog. Direct. No waste. Quietly confident.

## Words we use

- *Home* (preferred over "residence" in most contexts)
- *Architect*, *built in*, *renovated*, *materials*
- *Neighborhood*, *nearby*, *location*
- *Off-market*
- *Tour*, *showing*, *visit*
- *Legacy* (used sparingly, when earned)

---

## Words we never use

- *Luxury* in H1/H2 headlines (overused — show don't tell). Fine in metadata and SEO copy.
- *Stunning*, *gorgeous*, *amazing*, *incredible* — adjective inflation
- *Dream home*
- *Hot listing*
- *Exclusive opportunity*
- *Don't miss*, *limited time*, *act now*
- *Curated* — overused across every industry
- *Provenance* — sounds like a wine catalog
- *Stewardship* — paternalistic
- *Discretion* as a marketing word — we practice it; we do not advertise it
- *Esteemed*, *distinguished*, *bespoke*
- *The most personal investment of all* — aspirational fog
- Emoji of any kind in professional copy

## Copy formulas

### Property descriptions — 3-paragraph direct structure

1. **What this home is (2–3 sentences).** Year built, architect, key materials, standout feature. No adjective inflation.
2. **Where it lives (2 sentences).** Neighborhood, what's nearby, context.
3. **Why it matters (1–2 sentences).** Who the right buyer is, why this holds value.

**Example (EN):**
> Villa Aurora was designed by Chad Oppenheim in 2021 — poured concrete shell, Italian travertine throughout, 5,800 square feet across two levels with a 60-foot infinity pool.
>
> It sits in Sunny Isles Beach, four minutes from Bal Harbour Shops and a private marina. The neighborhood draws owners who want proximity to Miami without the density.
>
> Properties like this trade rarely. This is the first offering since completion.

Compare to the v1 four-paragraph version: same information, half the words, no ornament.

---

### Hero headlines

A good hero headline:
1. Is clear about what we do and where
2. Uses 4–7 words
3. Reads naturally in Inter weight 300 (not reliant on italic Playfair to work)

**Approved:**
- *Miami real estate, considered.*
- *Find a home worth keeping.*
- *Premium homes in South Florida.*
- *A modern approach to luxury real estate.*

**Rejected:**
- *Where luxury becomes home.* (v1 — aspirational fog)
- *The home is the most personal investment of all.* (v1 — too long, too precious)
- *Provenance you can come home to.* (v1 — sounds like a wine catalog)
- *Find your dream home today!* (cliché, urgent)
- *Premium luxury real estate in Miami* (SEO-stuffed)

---

### Eyebrows (the small label above the headline)

Inter 12px, natural case, neutral-600. No uppercase, no tracking, no gold.

Approved patterns:
- `South Florida`
- `Off-market listings`
- `Miami · South Florida`
- `Selected homes`

## Bilingual policy

### Translation philosophy

We do not translate word-for-word. We **transcreate** — every string in ES is independently crafted to feel native to a Spanish-speaking Miami resident, not translated from English.

The Spanish version uses **usted** (formal), neutral international Spanish. Avoid Mexican, Argentine, or Cuban regionalisms. Use standard Miami market real estate vocabulary (e.g. *casa* not *vivienda*, *condominio* not *piso*).

### Tagline pairs

| EN | ES |
|---|---|
| Miami real estate, considered. | Miami real estate, con criterio. |
| Schedule a tour | Agendar una visita |
| Browse Properties | Ver propiedades |
| Contact us | Contáctanos |
| Off-market access | Acceso fuera de mercado |
| Search | Buscar |

### Fallback rule

If a piece of content has no ES equivalent yet, the site shows the EN version with a small `[EN]` indicator. **Never machine-translate.**

## Visual art direction

### What our photography looks like

- **Architecture-first:** The building is the protagonist. People are absent or implied.
- **Natural light:** Golden hour or soft overcast. No harsh midday sun.
- **Composition:** Off-center, with negative space. Room to breathe.
- **Color treatment:** Natural tones. No warm crush, no oversaturation, no HDR.

### What our photography is NOT

- Smiling agents in suits
- Realtor signs in front of houses
- Drone shots that look like Google Earth
- HDR-blended interior shots that look fake
- Couples touring with realtors
- Stock photography of "luxury"

### Until we have real photography

Use:
- Solid neutral placeholders in off-white or neutral-200
- The Estate One triangle isotype as a watermark on placeholder cards

Do NOT use Unsplash for production. Mark all Unsplash usage with `// TODO: replace with commissioned photography`.

## When the brand contradicts a stakeholder request

If someone (the founder, a marketer, an investor) asks for something that contradicts this brand document — **flag it, don't just comply**. Examples:

- Request: "Add a popup with a discount code"
  - Response: "That conflicts with our direct, low-pressure approach. Could we instead [alternative]?"
- Request: "Use Unsplash photos for the launch"
  - Response: "Acceptable as a temporary placeholder, marked in code as TODO. Not for the public launch."
- Request: "Add testimonials from old clients (paraphrased)"
  - Response: "Testimonials must be real and attributed. If we can't get permission, we leave a placeholder."

The brand is the long-term asset. Short-term requests should bend to it, not the other way around.
